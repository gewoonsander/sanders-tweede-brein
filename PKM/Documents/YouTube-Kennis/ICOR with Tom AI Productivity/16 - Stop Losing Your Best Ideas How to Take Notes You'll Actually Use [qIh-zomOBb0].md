We call it the capturing beast and it ensures every note you take actually serves your goals. First, you understand the four types of information you capture. In ICO, we divide everything into four categories. PKM, personal knowledge management for personal information that only affects you, book insights, learning notes, personal ideas. BKM, business knowledge management for team information you need to share, meeting notes, decisions, process documentation. PPM, personal project management for your personal tasks. BPM, business project management for team tasks. When you capture a note, you immediately ask two questions. Is this personal or team? Is this information or action? That tells you where it belongs. For example, a book inside that you want to remember. That's PKM. A process improvement idea for your team, that's BKM. A task that came out of that idea, that's BPM. This removes the where do I save this paralysis completely. Second, use an intermediate capture tool for speed, then process to your single source of truth. When you're on mobile or need to capture something fast, use whatever's quickest. Apple Notes, voice memos, whatever. But there's the key. You process these notes later and move them to their proper home. Third, link every note to a project, goal, or task if it makes sense. Never save standalone notes floating in folders. If you're capturing feedback from a client call, link it directly to the client's task. This creates automatic context.
