## Part 1 — Platform Knowledge Digest

### 1. What Plug&Pay Is, Core Mental Model

Plug&Pay is a Dutch checkout/funnel-building SaaS: "een **product** in Plug&Pay is het item dat je verkoopt" (a product is the thing you sell) — online courses, e-books, coaching, physical goods, or subscriptions — separate from a **betaalpagina** (payment/checkout page), which is *how* you sell it. One product can power multiple payment pages; the product carries pricing/billing-type/VAT logic, the payment page carries the customer-facing design, copy, and conversion tooling (upsells, order bumps, countdowns). *(Source: [Producten uitgelegd](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/14183653-producten-uitgelegd))*

This Product ↔ Payment-Page split is the single most load-bearing structural concept for anyone building or debugging a Sander funnel — mirrors the Huddle brief's "Product → Module → Page" hierarchy as the equivalent backbone concept on that platform.

### 2. Products & Checkout Pages

**Creating a product** *(Source: [Een product aanmaken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4736842-een-product-aanmaken))*: Products > Create Product → (1) title + optional description + image → (2) Pricing: billing cycle (one-time / subscription / installment), price, VAT %, incl./excl. VAT → (3) optional AI-generated checkout page (target audience + product summary as prompt) → (4) optional AI-generated sales/landing page → done, further editable anytime.

**Three payment/billing types per product**, selected at creation and changeable later: **Eenmalig** (one-time), **Abonnement** (subscription), **Termijnbetaling** (installment). *(Source: [Producten uitgelegd](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/14183653-producten-uitgelegd), cross-checked against [Een product instellen als abonnement](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4736852-een-product-instellen-als-abonnement) and [Een product in termijnen laten betalen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4736934-een-product-in-termijnen-laten-betalen))*

**Creating a checkout page** *(Source: [Een betaalpagina maken in Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4737042-een-betaalpagina-maken-in-plug-pay))*: Pages > Payment Pages > Create Payment Page → select product, name, template, design theme → optional AI content generation. Editor tabs: **General** (core properties), **Content** (copy/images/messaging), **Design** (template/branding), **Pricing** (custom/dynamic/pay-what-you-want pricing), **Advanced** (country restrictions, VAT, custom JS), **Upsells** (post-purchase offers), **Integrations** (ActiveCampaign, webhooks, etc.). An AI page-builder option exists as an alternative entry point. *(Source: [Een betaalpagina maken met AI in Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8420441-een-betaalpagina-maken-met-ai-in-plug-pay))*

**Pricing flexibility on a checkout page**: custom one-off price override, a fully dynamic price (formula/parameter-driven), and "Betaal wat je wilt" (pay-what-you-want). *(Sources: [Een aangepaste prijs gebruiken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/11769137-een-aangepaste-prijs-gebruiken-op-de-betaalpagina), [Een dynamische prijs instellen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/12899114-een-dynamische-prijs-instellen), ['Betaal wat je wilt' gebruiken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/14033840-betaal-wat-je-wilt-gebruiken))*

**Order-summary / thank-you page ("besteloverzichtpagina")**: shown post-purchase, three implementation options — Plug&Pay's default built-in summary, a custom Plug&Pay landing page flagged as "order-summary" type (must include the mandatory order-data block; can add confirmation copy, video, CTAs, socials), or a fully external URL. **Known limitation, stated explicitly in the docs**: the same summary page shows regardless of which upsells/order bumps the customer bought — no conditional logic yet. *(Source: [Een besteloverzichtpagina gebruiken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/5102059-een-besteloverzichtpagina-gebruiken))*

**Volume/tiered discounting**: "Staffelkorting" lets a single product offer automatic price breaks at quantity thresholds — article exists, not deep-dived (lower priority per brief). *(Source: [Staffelkorting instellen voor je product](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/13055258-staffelkorting-instellen-voor-je-product))*

### 3. Subscriptions & Recurring Billing

**What a subscription is**: "a payment type whereby a customer pays an amount at fixed intervals, as long as the subscription is active" — billing cycles supported are **monthly, quarterly, yearly**; the customer picks their interval at checkout if multiple are offered. *(Source: [Abonnementen in Plug&Pay uitgelegd](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/15381579-abonnementen-in-plug-pay-uitgelegd))*

**Prerequisite**: subscriptions require SEPA Direct Debit or credit card as payment method (iDEAL/Bancontact only work for recurring billing once SEPA Direct Debit is activated on top) — Mollie or Stripe as payment provider, SEPA approval typically takes 2–4 days. *(Cross-checked across [Abonnementen uitgelegd](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/15381579-abonnementen-in-plug-pay-uitgelegd) and [Plug&Pay koppelen met Mollie](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8259897-plug-pay-koppelen-met-mollie))*

**Tier gate: Premium or Ultimate required, not available on Lite.** *(Source: [Een product instellen als abonnement](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4736852-een-product-instellen-als-abonnement); consistent with the official pricing page §14 below)*

**Setup flow**: Product → Pricing tab → select "Abonnement" → set price → after creation, Prices tab lets you add multiple billing cycles (e.g., add quarterly alongside monthly) and mark one as the highlighted/default option shown at checkout. Discount sub-options on the same tab: a straight promotional discount on the subscription price, an "introductory rate" (different price for the first billing period only), and a trial period (see below). "Price triggers" (Content > Price Triggers on the checkout page) can surface discount-percentage badges, per-month cost breakdowns, and "recommended" labels to nudge customers toward a preferred cycle. Active subscriptions are viewed/managed at **Sales > Subscriptions**. *(Source: [Een product instellen als abonnement](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4736852-een-product-instellen-als-abonnement))*

**Trial periods**: configured under the same Pricing tab's Discount dropdown → "Trial period" → set duration + price (typically €0). Requires SEPA Direct Debit to be active at Mollie/Stripe, because the customer must still enter an IBAN to authorize the auto-renewal that follows the trial. **Explicit limitation in the docs: Plug&Pay cannot auto-terminate a trial — if not cancelled, it silently converts into a paid subscription.** Trials are tracked at Sales > Subscriptions (labeled "Trial") and Statistics > Subscriptions > Trials (started/stopped/renewed breakdown). *(Source: [Een (gratis) proefperiode instellen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4868235-een-gratis-proefperiode-instellen))*

**Cancelling a subscription — admin side**: Sales > Subscriptions → select subscription → Actions block → two distinct end-states:
- **"Geannuleerd" (Cancelled)** — you set an end date and choose whether collections may still run until then; optional reason.
- **"Beëindigd" (Terminated)** — ends immediately; optional reason.

**Important gap, stated explicitly in the docs**: "No email is sent to your customer when the subscription is cancelled or terminated" via the admin-side flow — merchants must handle that communication themselves. *(Source: [Een abonnement annuleren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4900594-een-abonnement-annuleren))*

**Customer self-service cancellation** requires the Customer Portal to be active — see §11, including a precisely-quoted tier-gate carve-out for Huddle customers that resolves a discrepancy with the Huddle brief.

**VAT reverse-charging on an existing subscription**: a dedicated flow exists to retroactively apply "btw verleggen" (VAT reverse charge, for valid EU B2B VAT-ID customers) to an already-running subscription — article exists, not deep-dived. *(Source: [Btw achteraf verleggen bij een abonnement](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/14680237-btw-achteraf-verleggen-bij-een-abonnement))*

### 4. Installment Payments ("Termijnbetalingen")

Distinct product type from subscriptions — a **fixed total price split into a defined number of payments** (2–12 installments), not an open-ended recurring charge. Example given in the docs: 6 installments of €450 or 12 of €240 for the same product. Customer pays the first installment at checkout; "de volgende termijnen worden automatisch geïncasseerd" — subsequent installments auto-collect monthly, same payment-method requirement as subscriptions (SEPA Direct Debit / credit card / iDEAL & Bancontact-with-SEPA). **Tier gate: Premium or Ultimate, not Lite.** *(Sources: [Termijnbetalingen in Plug&Pay uitgelegd](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/15480470-termijnbetalingen-in-plug-pay-uitgelegd), [Een product in termijnen laten betalen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4736934-een-product-in-termijnen-laten-betalen))*

**Setup**: Product → Prices tab → Billing Cycle dropdown → "Termijnbetaling" → offer up to two installment-plan variants alongside a one-time ("Eenmalig") option → set installment count (2–12) and per-installment price → optional "Prijstriggers" banner nudging a specific plan. Active installment plans managed under the same Sales area as subscriptions; view/modify individual plans and cancellation both have dedicated articles ([Termijnbetalingen bekijken en wijzigen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/15532248-termijnbetalingen-bekijken-en-wijzigen), [Een termijnbetaling annuleren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8205305-een-termijnbetaling-annuleren)), not deep-dived but structurally mirror the subscription cancellation flow in §3.

### 5. Upsells, Order Bumps ("Kassakoopjes"), Downsells — the Post-Purchase Funnel

**Upsells**: a one-click supplementary offer shown *after* the initial payment succeeds but before the thank-you page — "Een upsell wordt pas getoond nadat de eerste betaling is gelukt." Because payment details are already captured, accepting charges the same method with zero re-entry. Funnels can chain multiple upsells (docs' own example: €47 initial → €77 upsell → €197 second upsell). **Requires Premium or higher; also requires SEPA Direct Debit or credit card active.** *(Sources: [Upsells uitgelegd](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/14854877-upsells-uitgelegd), [Een upsell aanmaken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4533726-een-upsell-aanmaken))*

**Creating an upsell**: Pages > Upsells > "Upsell aanmaken" (or the + icon inside a payment page's Upsells tab) → name + linked product → Type: Plug&Pay template / a landing page / your own external website → General tab (name, product, type, color, URL slug) → Content tab (titles, subtitles, description, video, images, USPs, testimonials, button text) → linked to the payment flow accordingly. Upsells can also attach to a landing page or to a product sold on your own external website. *(Sources: same as above, plus [Een upsell koppelen aan een landingspagina](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/11144741-een-upsell-koppelen-aan-een-landingspagina), [Een upsell koppelen aan een eigen website](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/5420598-een-upsell-koppelen-aan-een-eigen-website), [Een upsell maken met getimede video-aanbieding](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8237080-een-upsell-maken-met-getimede-video-aanbieding))*

**Kassakoopjes (order bumps)**: a cheap add-on shown *on* the checkout page itself, below the payment methods — explicitly compared in the docs to supermarket checkout impulse buys. Setup: Payment Page → Content tab → "Kassakoopje" → either add an existing product (customize title/description/color/image) or use AI "Suggestions" to auto-recommend one. **Hard cap: max 3 kassakoopjes per payment page.** *(Source: [Een kassakoopje aanmaken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/6799347-een-kassakoopje-aanmaken))* A related reminder feature exists to nudge customers who viewed but didn't add a kassakoopje ([Kassakoopje-herinnering instellen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8469963-kassakoopje-herinnering-instellen), not deep-dived).

**Downsells**: shown only when a customer *declines* an upsell — typically a cheaper/lighter alternative. **Can only be created once a linked upsell already exists** (you attach a downsell to an existing upsell via the "Link Existing Downsell" dialog on Payment Page > Upsells). **Requires Premium or Ultimate — not Lite.** *(Source: [Downsell instellen in Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/6768403-downsell-instellen-in-plug-pay))*

**Anti-pattern flag for whoever builds Sander's funnels**: kassakoopjes, upsells, and downsells are three structurally distinct mechanisms with different creation paths and different tier gates — conflating them (e.g., trying to set a "downsell" without an upsell existing first, or assuming kassakoopjes have no cap) is the most likely rookie mistake here.

### 6. Discount Codes

Tools > Kortingscodes > create → **Options**: name, type (fixed amount / percentage / free product), whether a fixed-amount discount applies to the whole order or per line-item (including order bumps), whether it recurs across every subscription/installment charge or only the first payment, the actual code string (custom or auto-generated). **Conditions**: restrict to one product or "all products," optional minimum order amount. **Restrictions**: once-per-email-address and/or total-use-cap, optional start/end dates. The discount-code input field must then be explicitly added to the checkout page. **Requires Premium or Ultimate — not Lite.** *(Source: [Kortingscode aanmaken en instellen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/3083749-kortingscode-aanmaken-en-instellen))* A separate toggle governs whether the code field even shows on a given checkout page ([Kortingscode toestaan op je betaalpagina](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/14675495-kortingscode-toestaan-op-je-betaalpagina)) and a dedicated flow exists for applying a code to an already-active subscription/installment plan ([Een kortingscode toepassen op abonnementen/termijnbetalingen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8212190-een-kortingscode-toepassen-op-abonnementen-termijnbetalingen)) — neither deep-dived.

### 7. Orders, Invoicing & VAT

**Order management**: Sales > Orders is the hub — view/manage all orders, manually create an order, view/send the associated invoice. *(Source: [Bestellingen bekijken en beheren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8072692-bestellingen-bekijken-en-beheren), [Overzicht van bestellingen in Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8071608-overzicht-van-bestellingen-in-plug-pay))*

**Viewing/sending a customer invoice**: open the order → "PDF Invoice" → Download (opens a new tab, savable as PDF) or Email (pre-fills subject/body from Settings > Email Templates > "Send Invoice," editable before sending). *(Source: [Klantfacturen bekijken en versturen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8102651-klantfacturen-bekijken-en-versturen))*

**Crediting an invoice**: order → Credit button → confirm (optionally auto-email the credit note to the customer). **"Het crediteren van een factuur kan niet ongedaan worden gemaakt"** — irreversible, stated explicitly. Result: order status flips to "Credited" and a separate order record holding the credit invoice is auto-generated. **Crediting is purely an administrative/bookkeeping action — the actual money refund is a separate manual step in Mollie or Stripe.** This is a genuine gotcha worth flagging proactively: crediting the invoice does not itself send money back to the customer. *(Source: [Een factuur crediteren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/6563530-een-factuur-crediteren))*

**Invoice settings** (Settings > Factuurinstellingen): numbering (prefix + minimum digit-length + auto-incrementing sequence, resets/continues per year), payment-term window for manual bank transfer (7–100 days), whether product descriptions appear on invoices, an auto-debit-notice toggle, and three customizable text blocks (automatic-debit text with order-ID/payment-link shortcodes, manual-transfer instructions with order-ID/payment-term shortcodes, and free-form footer text). A "Peppol activeren" article is cross-referenced but not itself detailed in this pass — flag as a gap if Sander ever needs Peppol e-invoicing specifics. *(Source: [Factuurinstellingen beheren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8612789-factuurinstellingen-beheren))*

**VAT/BTW settings** (Settings > Btw & Unieregeling): reporting country determines which VAT rates are even selectable per product (changing it forces re-assigning VAT % across the whole catalog — a non-trivial migration, worth flagging before anyone flips this lightly); a customizable 0%-VAT invoice description; an "international sales" toggle that auto-applies 0% VAT outside the EU (location detected via shipping address for physical goods, IP for digital); per-product VAT % + incl./excl. display set on the product's Pricing tab; automatic reverse-charging ("verleggen") for customers who enter a VIES-validated EU VAT ID (the VAT-ID input field itself must be separately enabled under Content > Fields, either for all orders or foreign-only); and OSS-regime guidance once EU-wide B2C sales exceed €10,000/year. *(Source: [Btw-instellingen beheren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/9357723-btw-instellingen-beheren))*

**Failed collections ("mislukte incasso's")**: Sales > Orders > "Failed Collections" tab lists unpaid/reversed direct-debit attempts, with cause (insufficient funds vs. bank-blocked/reversed) visible per order and a full attempt/email History > Activities timeline (Mollie/Stripe failure reasons surface here too, plus a "PSP status" deep-link into the provider dashboard). Plug&Pay **automatically retries failed collections and sends reminder emails** (template customizable at Settings > Emails > "Failed Collection"). **Explicit and important gap: subscriptions are NOT auto-cancelled after repeated failed payments — this requires manual intervention**, i.e. a churned non-paying subscriber can silently sit "active" with unresolved billing indefinitely unless someone checks this tab. Manual levers: Collect Again (unpaid only, not reversed), Send Reminder, or a shareable Payment Link (auto-updates order to Paid on success). For persistent non-payment, the docs point to either direct manual outreach or the (Ultimate-only) debtor system below. *(Source: [Mislukte incasso's beheren en opvolgen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4737141-mislukte-incasso-s-beheren-en-opvolgen))*

**Debtor/dunning system ("Debiteurensysteem")**: automated 4-step collection sequence — 3 regular reminders, then a 4th notice that adds dunning fees to the amount owed — plus a dashboard (outstanding invoices, revenue recovered, pipeline stage) and an optional integration with **Pingen** to physically mail dunning letters alongside the 3rd/4th reminder. **"The debtor system is part of Plug&Pay Ultimate. If you use Plug&Pay Lite or Premium? Upgrade to Plug&Pay Ultimate to use this feature"** — quoted near-verbatim from the docs, tier gate is explicit and unambiguous. *(Sources: [Debiteurensysteem instellen en gebruiken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/9656781-debiteurensysteem-instellen-en-gebruiken), [Plug&Pay koppelen met Pingen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/9656931-plug-pay-koppelen-met-pingen-automatisch-brieven-versturen-bij-herinneringen); tier gate corroborated by the official pricing page, §14)*

### 8. Testing & Data Export

Two testmodus articles cover placing a test order and following it through while test mode is active (not deep-dived — routine QA workflow, low ambiguity risk). A "Verkoopdata exporteren" article covers exporting sales data — not deep-dived. *(Sources: [Een testbestelling plaatsen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/3083756-een-testbestelling-plaatsen), [Bestelling tijdens testmodus opvolgen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8460855-bestelling-tijdens-testmodus-opvolgen), [Verkoopdata exporteren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8101381-verkoopdata-exporteren))*

### 9. Payment Providers: Mollie & Stripe

Plug&Pay itself is not a payment processor — it requires **Mollie or Stripe** underneath. *(Source: [Betaalproviders uitgelegd](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/14124792-betaalproviders-uitgelegd), not deep-dived beyond this framing)*

**Connecting Mollie**: Settings > Payment Provider > Connect → log into Mollie → confirm → default website profile applies automatically (pick manually if you run multiple Mollie profiles) → payment methods visible in Plug&Pay mirror what's enabled in the Mollie dashboard (Settings > Online Payments there). **SEPA Direct Debit must be separately enabled in Mollie for subscriptions/installments — approval takes roughly 2–4 days**, a real lead-time to plan around before launching a recurring-billing product. *(Source: [Plug&Pay koppelen met Mollie](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8259897-plug-pay-koppelen-met-mollie))*

**Connecting Stripe**: Settings > Payment Provider > select Stripe > authenticate > separate **test** connection and **live** connection must each be individually authorized (both should show green "Gekoppeld"). Supported methods: creditcard, iDEAL, SEPA Direct Debit, Bancontact. **Explicit, important warning: "Je kunt niet gemakkelijk switchen tussen Mollie en Stripe"** — switching providers breaks automatic invoice-status syncing and, critically, existing subscription/installment mandates (payment-authorization tokens) do not transfer between providers, which can silently break recurring billing for existing customers. Plug&Pay support must be contacted before attempting a live provider migration. *(Source: [Plug&Pay koppelen met Stripe](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/9576396-plug-pay-koppelen-met-stripe))* — **this is single-sourced within the help center** (no second Mollie/Stripe-switching article corroborates it), flagged as Medium confidence on the exact mechanics, though the general "provider switching is risky for recurring billing" claim is a well-known category of payment-industry behavior.

### 10. Forms & Lead Capture

Forms collect opt-ins or customer data — usable standalone (e.g. free-lead-magnet delivery, auto-emailing a file/link on submit) or as a funnel front-door. Six fields (first name, last name, address, phone, country, email) persist in-browser and auto-populate onto a subsequent checkout page in the same session. Feeds into: **Huddle** (temporary-access-level trials — a visitor registers via form with minimal friction before a trial window expires, per the Huddle brief's §3 access mechanism #2), **Zapier** (form-submission trigger fires downstream actions), and **ActiveCampaign/MailBlue** (list-building, automated sequences). *(Source: [Formulieren in Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/9051270-formulieren-in-plug-pay))* Field-building and spam-management have their own articles, not deep-dived. *(Sources: [Velden toevoegen aan een formulier](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/12688734-velden-toevoegen-aan-een-formulier), [Spam-inzendingen beheren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/13714147-spam-inzendingen-beheren))*

### 11. Customer Portal ("Klantenportaal") — Self-Service Subscription Management

**What it is**: a self-service hub where customers can download invoices/UBL files, manage subscriptions, and update contact info without merchant intervention. **Setup**: Settings > Customer Portal > Activate → configure URL/access link to share with customers, feature permissions, and appearance (logo, primary color, optional removal of "Powered by Plug&Pay"). *(Source: [Klantenportaal instellen en gebruiken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8613135-klantenportaal-instellen-en-gebruiken))*

**Tier gating — precisely quoted, cross-checked across three independent articles, and the resolution to a discrepancy flagged by Hermes's brief:**

The general Customer Portal article contains an explicit feature-by-tier comparison table:

| Capability | Lite | Premium | Ultimate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cancel an order (within 14 days of first invoice) | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Cancel/resume a subscription | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Change billing cycle | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Change invoice details | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Own logo + brand color | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

With the explicit caption: **"ℹ️ Het Klantenportaal heeft beperkingen in Plug&Pay Lite & Premium"** — i.e., self-service subscription cancellation/resumption is **Ultimate-only in the general case**. *(Source: [Klantenportaal instellen en gebruiken](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/8613135-klantenportaal-instellen-en-gebruiken))*

**But** a separate article on subscription cancellation states, quoted verbatim: **"Deze optie is alleen beschikbaar met Plug&Pay Ultimate of Plug&Pay Premium wanneer je gebruikmaakt van de Huddle-koppeling"** — i.e. there is an explicit **carve-out: Premium also unlocks customer self-service subscription cancellation, specifically when the Huddle integration is in use.** *(Source: [Een abonnement annuleren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/4900594-een-abonnement-annuleren))*

A third article, specific to Huddle members using the Customer Portal, independently states: **"Deze functie is onderdeel van Plug&Pay Premium & Ultimate"** — consistent with the carve-out (Premium is sufficient *for the Huddle-connected flow specifically*, not as a general statement about the Customer Portal). *(Source: [Huddle leden hun abonnement laten beheren via het Klantenportaal](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/9562194-huddle-leden-hun-abonnement-laten-beheren-via-het-klantenportaal))*

**Verdict — Confidence: High.** The three articles are not actually contradictory once read together: the *general* rule is Ultimate-only for subscription self-service in the Customer Portal; there is a *documented, Huddle-specific exception* where Premium also qualifies. This is corroborated by the official pricing page (§14), which lists "customer portal" as an Ultimate-tier feature with no Huddle caveat mentioned on the pricing page itself — the caveat lives only in the help-center articles.

**Cross-check against the Huddle brief §13**: `Deliverables/2026-07-14-huddle-specialist-hire-research.md` §13 states the Customer Portal is "exclusive to Plug&Pay Premium/Ultimate plans" — **this is directionally correct but imprecise**. It correctly flags this as "a Plug&Pay-side gate, separate from Huddle's own tiers," but collapses the Ultimate-vs-Premium+Huddle nuance into a flat "Premium/Ultimate" statement. **This brief refines rather than contradicts that finding**: for Sander's actual stack (Huddle-connected Dart Buddies), Premium is sufficient for the Customer Portal's subscription self-service, *because* the Huddle connection is active — but this would NOT hold for the standalone Gewoon Sander / Darttactiek checkout (no Huddle involved), where Ultimate would be required for members to self-serve subscription cancellation via the Customer Portal. **This distinction matters concretely for Sander's two businesses and should be flagged to whoever configures each shop's plan.**

### 15. Integrations — Webhooks, Zapier, Accounting Software, and the General Automation Layer

**The underlying rules engine**: Plug&Pay's automation model is a trigger → target → action rule, configurable at three scopes:
1. **Universal** (Settings > Connections / "Universele koppelingen") — fires on every occurrence of the trigger event platform-wide, regardless of product/page.
2. **Per-product** (Product > Koppelingen tab) — scoped to one product (this is what the Huddle-specific articles document, §16).
3. **Per-payment-page** (Payment Page > Integrations tab) — scoped to one checkout page.

Trigger events available: affiliate accepted/rejected/stopped, invoice created, order paid, subscription ended, installment activated/ended (exact set varies slightly by article — treated as the union). Targets: **Huddle** (access-level grant/revoke), **ActiveCampaign/MailBlue** (tagging/automation), or a **Webhook**. *(Sources: [Universele koppelingen toevoegen](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/12975327-universele-koppelingen-toevoegen), [Een webhook instellen in Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/3083761-een-webhook-instellen-in-plug-pay))*

**Failure recovery — operationally important**: failed rule executions show an orange warning triangle on the affected order; hovering reveals the error, and individual rules can be re-run via arrow buttons in the order's History > Connections tab. For bulk recovery: top-right menu > "Onverwerkte koppelingen" (Unprocessed Integrations) > "Alles opnieuw uitvoeren" (Retry All) — re-attempts every failed rule platform-wide (can take several minutes). Common failure causes cited: bad webhook URLs, or a target (access level/tag) that no longer exists. **This is the single most important troubleshooting lever for anyone debugging "customer paid but didn't get Huddle access" — worth surfacing proactively to Martonny or Sander as the first diagnostic step**, not something either specialist should have to rediscover under pressure. *(Source: [Onverwerkte koppelingen uitvoeren](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/12975405-onverwerkte-koppelingen-uitvoeren))*

**Webhooks (V2) specifics**: Settings > Integrations > Add Integration (universal) or Product/Page > Integrations tab (scoped) → name, trigger, action = "Webhook V2" → destination URL. Payload is a **thin event envelope only** — `trigger_type`, `triggerable_id`, `triggerable_type`, `sent_at`, `tenant_id`, `rule_id` — the actual order/subscription data is *not* included in the webhook payload itself and must be fetched separately via authenticated GET calls to `api.plugandpay.com/v2/orders/{id}` or `/v2/subscriptions/{id}`. Optional payload-signature verification key lives at Settings > Developers. **This "thin payload, second API call required" pattern is a concrete implementation detail anyone building a custom automation needs to know up front** — it's easy to assume (incorrectly) that the webhook itself carries full order data. *(Source: [Een webhook instellen in Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/3083761-een-webhook-instellen-in-plug-pay))*

**Zapier**: Plug&Pay is **trigger-only in Zapier — it cannot be used as a Zapier action** ("Het is niet mogelijk om Plug&Pay als action te gebruiken in Zapier," stated explicitly). Five available triggers: Affiliate Accepted, Form Submitted, Invoice Created, Order Paid, Subscription Ended. Trigger filters: by specific product, by product group, and a toggle for whether a recurring-billing trigger fires on every invoice or only the initial order. Requires a Plug&Pay API key (Settings > Developers) for the Zapier connection. *(Source: [Plug&Pay koppelen met Zapier](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/3080917-plug-pay-koppelen-met-zapier))* — this directionally matches the Huddle brief's §13 note that Huddle's own Zapier integration is separate and free-tier-limited (100 executions/month); **the two Zapier integrations (Plug&Pay's and Huddle's) are independent Zapier "apps" with their own trigger/action sets — do not assume a Zap built against one automatically covers the other.**

**Accounting software connections** (9 platforms documented, article titles only, not deep-dived — routine bookkeeping-sync setups, lower ambiguity risk, pull on demand): Moneybird ("directe koppeling," implying deeper/native integration than the others), MoneyMonk, Billit, e-Boekhouden.nl, Exact Online, Reeleezee, SnelStart, Twinfield, Visma eAccounting, Yuki.

**Other payment-adjacent integration**: Pingen (physical dunning letters, tied to the Ultimate-only debtor system, §7).

### 16. Huddle Integration — The Plug&Pay/Huddle Boundary (Read This Before Touching Anything Cross-Platform)

This is the connective tissue between this new specialist and Martonny. **Governing principle: Plug&Pay owns the commerce/billing side of the handshake — product, price, checkout, subscription lifecycle, invoicing, the Customer Portal. Huddle owns everything that happens once a user is inside the community/e-learning environment — access levels, content gating, the member-facing UI.** The integration is the bridge between the two, and it is configured almost entirely from the **Plug&Pay side** (rules live on the Plug&Pay product/page, not inside Huddle, except for the initial OAuth-style connection and the in-Huddle checkout button placement).

**Connecting the two platforms**: Plug&Pay > Settings > Plugins > Huddle block > enter Huddle domain → "Inloggen bij Huddle" (OAuth-style login) → "Verbinden" to finalize. Once connected, the Huddle plugin auto-activates inside the Plug&Pay environment. *(Source: [Plug&Pay koppelen met Huddle](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/3080953-plug-pay-koppelen-met-huddle))* — **this matches the Huddle brief §13 exactly, word for word on the mechanics** (both briefs cite the identical article — no discrepancy, High confidence).

**Granting access on purchase** — configured per-product under the product's **Koppelingen** tab (or universally, per §15): trigger = "Factuur aangemaakt" (invoice created) or "Bestelling betaald" (order paid) for one-time products, or "Abonnement ingegaan" for subscriptions → action = Huddle → assign one or more access levels, or "Voeg alleen Huddle gebruiker toe" (add user only, no access level). **Huddle-tier gate on this action (a Huddle-side gate, not a Plug&Pay-side gate)**: Huddle Premium/Ultimate can assign full access levels; **Huddle Lite can only add a user, cannot assign access levels** — consistent with the Huddle brief's §4 finding that Lite Huddle has no access-level system at all. Form submissions ("Formulier verzonden" trigger) work identically, useful for free/lead-magnet access. *(Source: [Toegang geven tot Huddle met Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/13486750-toegang-geven-tot-huddle-met-plug-pay))* — **matches the Huddle brief §13 exactly; no discrepancy.**

**Revoking access on cancellation** — same Koppelingen tab, two triggers: **"Abonnement geannuleerd"** (cancelled but still active until the end date — use this if access should be pulled immediately regardless of remaining paid time) vs. **"Abonnement geëindigd"** (subscription has actually gone inactive — use this if access should persist through any remaining paid period). Action = Huddle → remove specified access level(s). **Full Huddle account deletion is explicitly NOT automated by this flow — it remains a manual step.** *(Source: [Toegang verwijderen in Huddle met Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/13486765-toegang-verwijderen-in-huddle-met-plug-pay))* — **matches the Huddle brief §13 exactly; no discrepancy.** One nuance worth surfacing that neither brief's source article states outright but that follows logically from §3+§7 above: **because the admin-side "Beëindigd" cancellation sends no customer email, and because failed collections do NOT auto-cancel a subscription**, a lapsed non-paying member's Huddle access can persist unnoticed unless the "Abonnement geëindigd" trigger is actually wired up and someone is watching the Failed Collections tab. This is an operational risk worth flagging to Sander directly rather than assuming the automation is self-healing.

**In-Huddle checkout ("direct afrekenen")**: **Huddle Premium/Ultimate only** (a Huddle-side gate). A Huddle product's Access tab gets a "Sales" button → link to an existing Plug&Pay product/checkout or auto-create a new one → set which access level(s) the purchase grants (pulled in as "Gekoppelde toegangsniveaus" from Plug&Pay) → optional separate sales-page URL on the product's Data tab for pre-purchase context. Member without access sees an in-place buy button, checks out, returns to Huddle with access already granted; for **public** Huddles specifically, checkout also collects an email and auto-creates the Huddle account post-purchase. *(Source: [Direct afrekenen binnen Huddle met Plug&Pay](https://help.plugandpay.com/nl/articles/10155285-direct-afrekenen-binnen-huddle-met-plug-pay))* — **matches the Huddle brief §13 exactly; no discrepancy.**

**Customer Portal for Huddle members**: see full discrepancy-resolution in §11 above — **the one place where the two platforms' documentation, read carelessly, could be mis-summarized as contradictory.** The precise, cross-verified answer: Plug&Pay's Customer Portal generally requires **Ultimate**; there's a **Premium+Huddle-connection carve-out** documented in two independent Plug&Pay articles, quoted verbatim in §11. **This is a Plug&Pay-tier gate, not a Huddle-tier gate — Huddle's own Lite/Premium/Ultimate tiers are irrelevant to whether a member can reach the Customer Portal; what matters is which Plug&Pay plan the merchant is on, and whether Huddle is connected.** Setup requires the Customer Portal active on Plug&Pay (Settings > Customer Portal > Activate) AND the Huddle-Plug&Pay integration connected. Members reach it via Huddle profile > Account > Purchases → redirect to Plug&Pay's portal.

**Boundary summary — the load-bearing sentence for the new specialist's contract**: *if the question is "can a member cancel their own subscription," the answer's mechanics live entirely on the Plug&Pay side (Customer Portal tier gate); if the question is "what does a member see/lose access to when that happens," the answer lives entirely on the Huddle side (access-level removal, content gating). A competent answer to almost any cross-platform Sander question names which side of this line it's on before answering.*

## Part 2 — SOP-001 Hire-Research Answers: "Plug&Pay-specialist"

### What would the best-in-world version of this role actually do, day to day?

The best version of this role is the commerce-and-billing counterpart to Martonny: an internal platform SME whose entire job is "does Plug&Pay actually do X, exactly how, and on which of Sander's own plans." Day to day: fields quick capability lookups from other specialists or Sander ("can we auto-cancel a subscriber's Huddle access the moment their card fails?" → no, not automatically — failed collections don't auto-cancel subscriptions, see §7, so the honest answer is "only if you manually terminate or build a watch-process on the Failed Collections tab"); drafts checkout/pricing-page specs and billing-flow diagrams that trace to exact tab names and settings screens, not vibes; maintains a living map of which Plug&Pay tier (Lite/Premium/Ultimate) gates which capability for *each* of Sander's two shops (DartsCoaching/Dart Buddies and Gewoon Sander/Darttactiek may plausibly sit on different plans, given their different feature needs — subscriptions and Huddle integration for one, a single standalone product checkout for the other); and is the first stop for diagnosing "customer paid but something downstream didn't happen" by knowing the Onverwerkte Koppelingen retry mechanism cold. The world-class version treats "this isn't in the docs, let me check directly in the account or ask Plug&Pay support" as a valid, valuable answer — never a guess dressed up as fact.

### Core competencies

1. **Docs-first, tier-first.** Every claim about a Plug&Pay feature traces to a specific help article and, ideally, an exact settings path — and is immediately qualified with "on which Plug&Pay tier." Nearly every feature past the checkout basics (subscriptions, installments, upsells, downsells, discount codes, affiliate system, Customer Portal, debtor system, team users) is Premium- or Ultimate-gated; a competent answer never omits this.
2. **Two-tier-system fluency, never conflated.** Fluent in the fact that a cross-platform question (e.g. "why can't this member cancel their own subscription?") can be gated by *either* Plug&Pay's own tier (Customer Portal → Ultimate, or Premium+Huddle) *or* Huddle's own tier (access-level assignment → Premium/Ultimate) — and correctly identifies which one is actually in play before answering. Conflating "Plug&Pay Premium" with "Huddle Premium" is the single most damaging failure mode this role can commit, mirroring the equivalent anti-pattern named in Martonny's contract.
3. **Billing-mechanics precision.** Correctly distinguishes subscriptions (open-ended recurring) from installment payments (fixed total, fixed number of charges) from one-time — and knows the operational gotchas that live in the gaps: crediting an invoice doesn't refund money (§7), failed collections don't auto-cancel (§7), cancelling a subscription sends no customer email (§3), Stripe/Mollie provider-switching breaks existing recurring mandates (§9).
4. **Automation/integration structural fluency.** Knows the three-scope rule engine (universal / per-product / per-page), the thin-webhook-payload pattern requiring a second API call, and — critically — the Onverwerkte Koppelingen retry mechanism as the default first diagnostic step for any "purchase succeeded but downstream action didn't fire" report.
5. **Distinguishes documented-and-stable from thin/gapped documentation.** Explicitly flags where Plug&Pay's own docs are thin (e.g., no in-help-center tier-comparison table, no dedicated API reference, Peppol only mentioned in passing) rather than presenting a reconstructed answer at the same confidence as a directly-documented one.

### Anti-patterns to explicitly avoid

- **Confident guessing on tier gates.** The number one failure mode — stating "yes, Plug&Pay can do that" without naming which tier it requires. This is exactly as damaging here as it is for Martonny's Huddle domain, because Sander runs two shops that may sit on different Plug&Pay plans; a tier-blind answer can send someone down a path that doesn't exist on the actual plan in use.
- **Conflating Plug&Pay's own tiers with Huddle's tiers.** Explicitly called out in the task brief and re-confirmed by this research: the two platforms both use a Lite/Premium/Ultimate naming scheme, which makes this conflation especially easy to make by accident. Every cross-platform answer must name which platform's tier is the actual gate (§16's "boundary summary" sentence is the standing rule).
- **Treating a webhook payload as if it carries full order data.** A specific, concrete trap documented in §15 — the payload is a thin event envelope; a second authenticated API call is required. Assuming otherwise breaks real automations, not hypothetically.
- **Assuming crediting an invoice refunds money, or that a cancelled subscription auto-notifies the customer.** Both are explicit, stated-in-the-docs gotchas (§7, §3) that a shallow reading would miss.
- **Scope creep into billing/business decisions.** This role supplies facts about what Plug&Pay can do and on which plan — not which plan Sander should buy, whether to run an affiliate program, or how to price a product. That's Sander's call, informed by this role's facts.
- **Owning or second-guessing the Huddle side of the integration.** When a cross-platform question is actually about what happens *inside* Huddle after access changes (content gating, member UI, notifications), this role hands it to Martonny rather than answering from Plug&Pay-side inference. The reverse holds too — Martonny should hand billing/checkout-mechanics questions back here.

### Deliverables this role should produce

- **Feature capability lookups** — cited, tier-aware answers to "can Plug&Pay do X," format: capability → yes/no/partial → exact settings path → Plug&Pay tier gate (and, where relevant, whether a Huddle-side gate also applies) → source article link.
- **Billing-flow specs** — docs-grounded descriptions of exactly what happens end-to-end for a given purchase type (one-time / subscription / installment), including the failure paths (declined card, failed collection, cancellation) and what is and isn't automated at each step — for Sander or another specialist to build a checkout/funnel around.
- **Cross-platform boundary maps** — for any Huddle-adjacent question, a short "this half is Plug&Pay's job, this half is Martonny's job" breakdown before diving into either side, per the §16 boundary principle.
- **Tier-gap audits** — proactively flagging when a requested capability requires upgrading DartsCoaching's or Gewoon Sander's actual current Plug&Pay plan, before anyone designs a flow around a feature that plan doesn't include.
- **Gap flags** — surfacing when a Sander request depends on undocumented behavior, a thin/reconstructed doc area (no canonical tier table, no API reference located), or genuinely ambiguous help-center wording (per §17), rather than filling the gap with a plausible-sounding guess.
- **World-class vs. adequate**: adequate output answers the literal question. World-class output also flags the adjacent operational trap nobody asked about — e.g., "yes, you can set up automatic Huddle-access-removal on cancellation — but note that a failed direct debit does NOT auto-cancel the subscription, so a non-paying member's access will persist unless you also watch the Failed Collections tab or wire the 'Abonnement geëindigd' trigger explicitly."

### Boundaries this role should hold

- Does not make pricing, plan-tier, or business decisions (which Plug&Pay plan to buy, whether to enable the affiliate system, how to price installments) — surfaces facts and tradeoffs, hands the decision back to Sander.
- Does not own or answer questions about what happens *inside* Huddle once access changes — that boundary belongs to Martonny. This role's Huddle-adjacent scope stops at the Plug&Pay-side configuration (the Koppelingen tab, the Customer Portal, the checkout-to-Huddle handoff) documented in §16.
- Does not write final-facing checkout copy, sales-page content, or marketing funnels — supplies the verified platform mechanics and constraints to whoever (Sander, or a future content/design specialist) builds the actual page.
- Does not guess at undocumented behavior and present it as fact — says "not documented, needs direct confirmation with Plug&Pay support" when the help center is silent, exactly as Martonny's contract requires for Huddle.
- Does not treat third-party review sites as equivalent-confidence to Plug&Pay's own docs for platform-mechanics questions — external sources are fine for pricing/market-positioning cross-checks (as used in §14) but not for "how does this feature actually work."
